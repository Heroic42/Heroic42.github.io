Jujitsu Basic Philosophy

Title Page Jujitsu Philosophy Jujitsu Techniques

The history of jujitsu begins in feudal Japan, as an art for samurai warfare.

In the modern age, the principles of ancient combat have been modified to suit

multiple different purposes aside from warfare. The table below explains each

jujitsu philosophy and what their uses are for

Philosophy Explanation Particulars
****************** ******************************************** ************************************************************************
Warfare Use of martial techniques for war Personal, unarmed or armed combat between nations meant to kill or maim
Sport Martial techniques used in a ring for entertainment Unarmed, techniques meant to submit or gain points
Law Enforcement Martial techniques used to aid in the arrest of criminals Unarmed, techniques used to subdue and take down
Personal Security Martial techniques used to defend another person Unarmed or armed, techniques used to quickly divert attackers from quarry
Personal Defense Martial techniques used to defend oneself non-lethally Primarily unarmed, techniques used to submit but not maim an attacker
Personal Development Martial techniques used for development of self Unarmed, techniques meant to teach discipline and peace
